Iced Teas Recipes

Chamomile Tea

What you will need:

  • Chamomile Flowers : 2 teaspoons
  • Water : 8 ounces
  • Tea Infuser

Instructions:

Step 1: Place chamomile flowers in a tea infuser

Place the chamomile flowers in a tea infuser and place it in a cup.

Step 2: Add water to the chamomile flowers

Add about 8 ounces of warmed water, around 200°F, to the cup. Let it steep for 5 - 7 minutes.

Step 3: Remove the tea infuser

Set aside. You can reuse the chamomile for a second steeping, but flavour will be milder than the first infusion. To get the taste you want, extend the steeping process accordingly.

Step 4: Add to a cup

Enjoy after a meal!

Peppermint Tea

What you will need:

  • Peppermint Leaves : 1 large sprig fresh Peppermint
  • Water : 1 cup
  • Kettle/Microwave
  • Strainer

Instructions:

Step 1: Heat water

Heat your water in a kettle or the microwave until just about boiling. [Around 200°F]

Step 2: Pour the water over the mint leaves in a cup

Pour the water over the mint leaves. Stir and gently bruise the mint leaves against the side of the cup to help release the flavour and let steep 2 minutes.

Step 3: Add sweentner and strain

Sweeten with honey or sugar if desired and enjoy. Mint is soothing for the tummy and relaxing for the soul. Enjoy!

Ginger Tea

What you will need:

  • Ginger : A few thinly sliced ginger
  • Water : 1 cup
  • Strainer
  • Saucepan

Instructions:

Step 1: Thinly slice ginger

Thinly slice your fresh ginger. You don’t need to peel it first, but do rinse it and scrub off any visible dirt. Plan on about using about a one-inch piece of ginger per cup of tea.

Step 2: Add ingredients into saucepan

In a saucepan, combine the ginger with fresh water (use one cup of water per serving). Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat. Reduce the heat as necessary to maintain a gentle simmer.

Step 3: Simmer the mixture for 5 minutes

Simmer for five minutes (or up to 10 minutes, if you want extra-strong tea). I usually think it’s pungent enough at five minutes.

Step 4: Strain the mixture into a cup

Pour the tea through a fine sieve to catch all of the ginger. If desired, serve your tea with a thin round of lemon or orange for some complementary acidity. You might also appreciate a light drizzle of honey or maple syrup, which will temper the fiery ginger flavor. Enjoy!
